# Monetisation Model
## Exposure Alpha 0.3

Exposure uses AI conversations, which create ongoing operating costs. Monetisation should cover AI usage without making the game feel unfair.

## Principles

- No forced ads.
- No pay-to-win murder solving.
- Premium improves session length, convenience and depth.
- Rewarded ads are optional and framed naturally in-world.
- AI limits should feel like gameplay limits, not billing limits.

## Recommended Model

### Free Tier

- Standard daily Focus/stamina allowance.
- Limited AI replies per scene.
- Limited save slots.
- Optional rewarded ads.

### Rewarded Ads

Rewarded ads can provide:

- Focus recovery
- Extra investigation action
- Extra AI replies for the current scene
- Coffee break stamina bonus
- Small time or convenience bonuses

In-world framing:

> Take a coffee break.

### Premium Subscription

Premium should include:

- No ads.
- Higher daily Focus/stamina.
- Higher AI replies per scene.
- Longer NPC memory.
- More save slots.
- Early access to new seasons.
- Cosmetic phone and case-board themes.

### Paid Expansions

Future revenue:

- Season passes.
- Bonus cases.
- Side mysteries.
- Cosmetic packs.

## Cost Controls

Backend controls:

- Maximum player message length.
- Maximum AI reply length.
- Maximum AI replies per scene.
- Maximum AI replies per day.
- NPC Focus gating.
- Memory compression.
- Cheaper models for normal NPC dialogue.
- Stronger models only for Director/Killer moments.

## Agent Focus as Cost Control

Each NPC has Focus. Focus controls how open they are to talking.

Conversation costs:

- Casual question: low Focus cost.
- Case question: medium Focus cost.
- Emotional question: medium/high Focus cost.
- Secret request: high Focus cost.
- Accusation: high Focus cost and possible Exposure increase.

When NPC Focus is low, the NPC naturally ends the conversation.

Example:

> I cannot talk about this right now. Find me later.

This keeps costs controlled while feeling like character behaviour.
